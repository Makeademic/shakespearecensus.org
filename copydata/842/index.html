Copy Details for 1 Henry IV (1632)

SC #: 254

Location: Sir John Soane's Museum

Shelfmark: GL32B

Copy-specific notes: Some pages badly frayed at the head, prior to binding.

Provenance notes: On H1r: 'Bala / Jones'; on I2v: 'Dear brother / I make Bould to [---?] / [---?] lines to you'; doodle on I2v; on I3r: 'Richard Anwylt his booke' and 'Mi[ss] Janet / Anwyll / his Booke'; on K1r: 'Richard [Anwy?]'. Probably acquired as one of a group of quarto plays printed around 1630, all in similar bindings, and bearing similar ownership inscriptions and marks suggesting they may have been previously bound together and broken up for separate sale.

Marginalia: Various early ink annotations including childish alphabet practice and marks of ownership partly lost in trimming of the fore-edges. 'Bullinbrook' inscribed in ink on C1v and 'Bullingbrooke' on C2r.

Binding: Late C18th or early C19th half calf, cerise paste-paper boards, gilt double-ruled spine with title and date in gilt.
